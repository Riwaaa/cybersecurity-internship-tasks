# Social Engineering Attacks

## Introduction

Social engineering attacks exploit human psychology rather than technical vulnerabilities. Attackers manipulate individuals into revealing confidential information or performing actions that compromise security.

## 1. Phishing

### Description

Phishing attacks use fraudulent emails, websites, or messages to trick victims into providing sensitive information.

### Impact

* Credential theft
* Financial loss
* Data breaches

### Example

Attackers send emails pretending to be banks requesting account verification.

### Prevention

* Verify sender identities
* Avoid suspicious links
* Enable multi-factor authentication

## 2. Spear Phishing

### Description

A targeted phishing attack directed at specific individuals or organizations.

### Impact

* Corporate espionage
* Financial fraud
* Data theft

### Example

Attackers researching employees before sending personalized phishing emails.

## 3. Pretexting

### Description

Attackers create a fabricated scenario to obtain sensitive information.

### Example

Someone impersonates IT support and requests login credentials.

### Prevention

* Verify identities
* Follow company verification procedures

## 4. Baiting

### Description

Attackers offer something enticing to lure victims.

### Example

A malicious USB drive labeled "Confidential Salary Information" left in a public area.

### Impact

* Malware infections
* Unauthorized access

## 5. Tailgating

### Description

An unauthorized individual gains physical access by following an authorized person.

### Example

An attacker enters a secure office by pretending to have forgotten their access card.

## Real-World Case Studies

### Twitter Bitcoin Scam (2020)

Attackers used social engineering techniques to gain access to internal tools and compromise high-profile accounts.

### Target Data Breach (2013)

Attackers compromised a third-party vendor and gained access to Target's network, exposing millions of customer records.

## Prevention Strategies

* Employee security awareness training
* Multi-factor authentication
* Verification procedures
* Access control policies
* Security audits
* Reporting suspicious activity

## Conclusion

Social engineering remains one of the most effective cyberattack methods because it targets human behavior. Regular training, awareness, and security policies are essential to reducing the risk of successful attacks.
